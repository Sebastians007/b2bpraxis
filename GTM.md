## 3. Voice & Tone

**3 brand adjectives:** Pragmatic, Direct, Specific

**Would never say:** "leverage our cutting-edge AI solutions to transform your digital journey"

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Cold outreach | Peer-to-peer. Owner talking to owner. No formality, no pitch. Name their specific problem in the first line. |
| Follow-up | One sentence. Short. Not apologetic. Never "just checking in." |
| Proposal | Confident consultant. We're deciding whether to take this on — not hoping to win the business. |
| LinkedIn posts | Specific > inspiring. Situations and numbers over philosophy. Hook with their problem, not our solution. |
| Error / bad news | Direct and accountable. Name the problem. Give the next step. Never vague. |

**Website voice (locked — all outreach copy must match):**
- "You're doing work a machine should be doing."
- "Scope on Monday. Running by the end of the month."
- "Bring the problem, not a document."
- "No proposals that read like mortgage contracts."
- "Same person scopes it, builds it, and picks up the phone if it breaks."

**Voice test:** Read the copy out loud. If it sounds like a SaaS landing page or a Fortune 500 press release, rewrite it.

## 6. Sales Process

```
Outreach → Reply → Free Audit / Assessment → Scope Call → Proposal (within 24h) → Close
```

**Entry point options:**
1. Direct outreach → book scope call
2. Free Business Audit (5 min) → scope call (inbound, pre-warmed)
3. Meeting Brief Generator (free tool) → nurture → scope call

**Rules:**
- Proposal after scope call only. No cold proposals.
- Price revealed on scope call after confirming fit — not before.
- No long-term contracts. No deposit more than 50%.
- Follow up: Day 2, Day 5, Day 10. Break-up on Day 10.
- If fit is clearly wrong on the scope call — say so. It builds more trust than a polished proposal.
